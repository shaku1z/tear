# C16 — TearBot Ladder and Agent Foundry

## Outcome

Historical scaffold result only. C16 established synthetic level formulas and
champion/challenger decision contracts. The Foundry currently receives a
prebuilt challenger and score dictionary from its caller; it does not train,
persist, schedule, or execute a challenger against real Tear gameplay.
Operational completion is reopened by C35-C36 of the autonomous completion
plan.

## Delivered

- Multidimensional Astuteness Vector and bounded-rationality compiler.
- Human-like information/reaction/planning firewall for public Levels 1–9.
- Orthogonal game difficulty, mechanical skill, strategic astuteness, and QA aggression.
- Provisional synthetic Levels 1–9 and explicit privileged Level Omega.
- Multidimensional scenario item-response probabilities.
- Monotonicity, adjacent separation, human-likeness, domain, weapon, mode, and difficulty reports.
- Separately consented human anchoring.
- Full Foundry weakness, curriculum, teacher, challenger, holdout, promotion, archive, monitoring, and rollback cycle.
- Frozen rewards, invariants, and release exams.

## Evidence

- Nine public levels were strictly ordered with adjacent separation on 100 hidden items.
- Level 9 stayed within human-like observation, reaction, and planning limits.
- Omega is labeled `PRIVILEGED / NON-HUMAN` and is not public.
- A reproducible Foundry cycle improved the weakest blade domain, promoted the challenger, detected later degradation, and rolled back.
- A reward-modifying challenger was rejected.

## Corrected Decision

Retain C16 as contract/prototype evidence. Plateau P5 is not complete. Promotion
requires executed-policy calibration and one durable autonomous Foundry cycle
that creates, trains, evaluates, promotes, monitors, and rolls back its own
challenger.
